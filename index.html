About Me

Hello! I'm Carmine Elvezio.

Hello! I'm a PhD Student at the Computer Graphics and User Interfaces Lab at Columbia University, studying AR/VR/XR, 3D graphics, interactions and visualization techniques, under the supervision of Prof. Steven Feiner.

Currently, with my research team, I am developing real-time interactive systems for urban visualization and planning, non-interventional medical procedures, simulation of various degenerative visual impairments, notifications in immersive environments, immersive diagnostic and rehabilitative games for patients of neuro-degenerative diseases, enhanced lighting simulations in AR, and several frameworks designed to facilitate the creation of these systems.

I work with a number of technologies including Microsoft HoloLens, Oculus Rift, Unity, Unreal, OpenGL, and many more!

Projects

I work on projects across a number of different domains and technologies, with many students and companies.

  • All
  • AR
  • VR
  • Robotics

ICThroughAR

The first medically-informed, pilot-studied simulation of cataracts in eye-tracked augmented reality (AR)

MiXR

A novel interactive system that combines an AR headset, a smartphone, and a tablet to allow performers to intuitively and efficiently manage and annotate virtual sheet music in their physical environment

SpaceLine

Showing how graphical cueing and precueing can be used to indicate the trajectories in a series of path-following tasks and discuss how performance can be improved by utilizing the look-ahead capability provided by precueing

CURVE

Interaction and visualization techniques in an AR/VR urban visualization system- intended to facilitate the comprehension, organization, and manipulation of data visualized in the spatial context in which it was generated.
